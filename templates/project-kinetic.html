Kinetic Typography Animation

Animation • Motion Design
IAT100 – Final Individual Project (Simon Fraser University)

Overview

This project explores how typography and motion can be used together to express emotion and rhythm. The animation is based on a speech clip and focuses on timing, contrast, movement, and visual storytelling.

Tools

Adobe After Effects, Premiere Pro

Process

This project started with selecting an audio clip that contains fast-paced speech and numerical information. Because the content is dense, I chose to structure the animation using a timeline-based layout to improve clarity and flow. This decision helped organize the information visually while matching the rhythm of the audio.

To make the animation more engaging, I focused on motion as a key visual element. I designed line animations that move along different paths and gradually form shapes, which helps guide the viewer’s attention across the screen.

After establishing the visual system, I synchronized the text with the audio. Different animation styles were applied to words, including fading, bouncing, and sliding, to create variation and rhythm. I also used the camera tool in After Effects to create movement such as zooming and scene transitions, adding depth to the overall composition.

I went through several iterations to refine timing, transitions, and readability. Early versions were either too fast to follow or too visually static, so I adjusted pacing and spacing between elements. These iterations helped balance clarity with the fast rhythm of the audio.

Challenges

One of the biggest challenges was matching the speed of the audio. Because the speech is very fast, many words and letters needed to appear in a short time. This required creating multiple subtitle layers and carefully adjusting timing in the timeline.

Another challenge was learning how to use the camera in After Effects. Controlling movement across different axes was difficult at first, and required repeated testing to achieve smooth and intentional motion.

Design Decisions

I used contrast in size, weight, and position to highlight key information. Line motion was used to connect elements and guide attention across the screen. A clean sans-serif typeface was chosen to improve readability and keep the overall design simple and clear.

Reflection

This project helped me understand how typography, motion, and camera movement work together in animation. I learned that small changes in timing and transitions can strongly affect how the audience experiences the work.

If I continue improving this project, I would refine the pacing further and simplify some sections to improve clarity. This project reflects my interest in motion design and my ability to combine visual storytelling with technical tools.
