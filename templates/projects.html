Projects

This page includes two projects I’ve worked on, focusing on storytelling and game design.

Kinetic Typography Animation

A kinetic typography animation based on a dialogue clip, focusing on how motion, timing, and typography can communicate emotion and tone.

▶ Watch on YouTube

Process

This project explores how kinetic typography can communicate emotion through motion and timing. The animation is based on a dialogue clip, and focuses on translating the tone, pacing, and emotional shifts of the voice into visual form.

The design emphasizes the relationship between sound and movement. As the dialogue progresses, the typography responds through changes in scale, position, and rhythm. Key words are highlighted through larger size and more dynamic motion, while quieter moments are expressed through slower pacing and minimal movement.

Timing plays an important role in the animation. The appearance and movement of the text are closely aligned with the rhythm of the speech, and pauses are reflected through moments of stillness. Layout and spacing are also used to guide attention and help the viewer follow the flow of the dialogue.

Through this project, I learned how typography can function not only as text, but also as a visual and expressive medium. It strengthened my understanding of how motion and timing can enhance storytelling.

Process Analysis

This animation was created as a final project for a previous course. I started by searching for suitable dialogue clips that could carry strong emotion and tone, and then developed the animation in After Effects and Premiere Pro.

One of the main challenges was learning After Effects from scratch. When trying to achieve certain visual effects, I often did not know the correct terminology, which made finding tutorials difficult. I solved this by describing effects in plain language when searching, then refining my searches once I learned the proper terms. I also asked classmates for help when needed.

Through this process, I experienced both the difficulty of producing animation independently and the satisfaction of solving problems step by step. It also improved my ability to connect motion, timing, and emotion in visual storytelling.
